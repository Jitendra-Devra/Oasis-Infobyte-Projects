Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India"

Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He was an aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Kalam was deeply involved in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts, earning him the title of the "Missile Man of India". He played a pivotal role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, establishing India as a nuclear power. Dr. Kalam was also known for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to education and youth. He passed away on 27th July 2015, leaving behind a legacy of inspiration and innovation.

Achievements

  • Played a key role in India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • Chief architect of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997.
  • Author of several inspirational books including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds".
  • Served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Quotes

"You have to dream before your dreams can come true."
"Excellence is a continuous process and not an accident."
